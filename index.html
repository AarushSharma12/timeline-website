Fall 2023 Cli-Fi: Climate Fiction

Week 1-3

"Have You Seen This Movie?"

We read the story Have You Seen This Movie by Allen Ginsberg. It was written as essentially jibberish but after some analysis proved to be an interesting critique on environmentalism. We also read Writing Down the Bones by Natalie Goldberg. The excerpts offer a unique approach to writing that emphasizes spontaneity, self-expression, and finding one's voice.

Week 4

Hope

We watched Greta Thunberg's "Our House is on Fire" speech, delivered at the 2019 World Economic Forum in Davos which emphasized the urgent need for decisive action to address the climate crisis, likening the state of the planet to a house on fire. We also read pieces by Rebecca Solnit that explored various successful environmental movements and initiatives, highlighting the power of collective action and the potential for positive change.

Week 5

Blame, Responsibility, and Guilt

In our first big project, we were tasked to choose an item and make a visualization of its environmental impact. We made drafts, presented them in small groups, and recieved feedback. We also watched an episode from "The Breakdown" which is a series focused on exploring key issues related to the climate crisis.

Week 6

Survival, Scarcity, and Accumulation

Week 7

Scarcity, Abundance, and Good Life

We read "Climate Changed" by Philippe Squarzoni which is notable for its accessible approach to a complex topic, using the graphic novel format to visually represent and simplify scientific concepts. It's a blend of personal memoir, investigative journalism, and scientific reportage, offering a unique and compelling perspective on one of the most critical issues of our time. We also did a Shot Sequence analysis of a scene from Three Robots using last weeks skills.

Week 8

The Role of Corporations

This week we read Charlie Jane Anders' "The Day it All Ended" and Paulo Bacigalupi's "The Calorie Man". "The Day It All Ended" (2014) by Charlie Jane Anders is a short science fiction story that explores themes of consumerism, environmental degradation, and the unexpected consequences of technological advancement. "The Calorie Man" is a short story set in a future where biotechnology and genetic engineering have led to major environmental and societal changes.

Week 9

Food, Thanksgiving, and Indigenous Peoples

We watched Bong Joon Ho's Snowpiercer to prepare for class this week. The film is set in a post-apocalyptic world where a failed climate-change experiment has killed off all life on the planet except for the inhabitants of the Snowpiercer, a train that travels around the globe powered by a perpetual-motion engine. The train is divided into strict class segments, with the wealthy living in luxury at the front and the poor, oppressed masses crammed into the tail. This week we also celebrated Thanksgiving and Native American Heritage Day.
